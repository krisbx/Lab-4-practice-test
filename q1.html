... Separating content from presentation

Upon some research, there appears to be several issues with the <b> and <i> tags. The most known one is the accessibility problem, where screen readers struggle to interpret the text within the tags. This leads to a loss of context for those with visual or other reading impairments. This leads into the fact that the <b> and <i> tags lack semantic meaning. They are known for being purely visual, whereas <strong> and <em> tags include the context, or meaning of the enclosed text. The last reason is a bit more niche, but by using <b> and <i> over <strong> and <em>, once your code becomes lengthy and requires updates, you may lose the image of what text is "visually" bold, and what text is actually important.
